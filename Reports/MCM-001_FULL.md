# 1. Overview

The Mars Cargo Module (MCM) system is the standardized container architecture for Mars Division logistics.  
It mirrors the Lunar LCM concept but is hardened for:

- Martian atmospheric Entry–Descent–Landing (EDL) loads  
- Extreme thermal cycling (−140°C to +30°C)  
- Dust infiltration resistance  
- Robotic handling under 0.38g  
- High‑density loading aboard MB‑Class barges  
- Compatibility with MTS‑1, MTS‑2, and MTS‑3 shuttles  

MCMs enable industrial‑scale cargo transport to Mars via a cyclic barge system.

---

# 2. MCM Family Overview

Three container classes:

## 2.1 MCM‑S (Small)
- Mass capacity: **1–2 tons**  
- Compatible with MTS‑1 and MTS‑2  
- Used for robotics, tools, sensors, small prefab components  

## 2.2 MCM‑M (Medium)
- Mass capacity: **5–8 tons**  
- Primary container for MTS‑2  
- Structural modules, solar pallets, ISRU components  

## 2.3 MCM‑L (Large)
- Mass capacity: **10–20 tons**  
- Used by MTS‑3  
- Habitat sections, power units, cryo tanks, heavy machinery  

All use the same interface block, latch geometry, and robotic handling rails.

# 3. Structural Design

## 3.1 Frame
- Titanium‑aluminum hybrid frame  
- Reinforced to survive 5–8 g EDL loads  
- Dynamic crush protection at bottom rails  

## 3.2 Shell
- Carbon composite panels with thermal barrier coating  
- Dust‑tight panel seams  
- Anti-static outer layer to reduce Martian dust adhesion  

## 3.3 Dimensions

### MCM‑S
- 1.4 m × 1.4 m × 1.8 m  

### MCM‑M
- 2.6 m × 2.6 m × 3.8 m  

### MCM‑L
- 5.0 m × 5.0 m × 6.5 m  

(Exact dimensions tuned to MTS cargo bay geometry.)

# 4. EDL Reinforcement Package

MCMs survive atmospheric entry inside MTS shuttles, but require:

- Shock‑isolated mounting posts  
- Thermal isolation blankets  
- Vibrational damping rails  
- Secondary thermal shield for sensitive cargo  

These features ensure container integrity after EDL dynamic loads.

---

# 11. Logistics Flow

1. MCMs loaded at OSY with mission cargo  
2. Transferred to barge by orbital tug  
3. Barge hauled to Mars via interplanetary tugs  
4. MTS shuttles ferry containers to surface  
5. MRCD robots unload and sort  
6. Empties returned to barge  
7. Barge returns to OSY for reloading  

A fully cyclic interplanetary logistics chain.
