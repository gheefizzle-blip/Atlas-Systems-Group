# 1. PURPOSE  

Part D concludes the transmission-asset evaluation series by addressing the **regulatory, environmental,  
airspace, tribal, federal land-use, and operational risks** associated with developing the  
15,360‑acre Heber North parcel into a **220 MW → 1.5 GW autonomous hydrogen-solar-launch facility**.  

This section provides an actionable, ATB-ready **risk register**, **mitigation strategy**, and **federal/state/county coordination plan** necessary for the next phase of acquisition and permitting.

# 2. NEPA ENVIRONMENTAL SCREENING (BASELINE)  

This project will ultimately trigger a **NEPA action**, likely an **Environmental Assessment (EA)** rather  
than a full **Environmental Impact Statement (EIS)** due to:  
- Non-extractive land use  
- Minimal water consumption (closed-loop RO + recycling)  
- Limited wildlife displacement  
- No hazardous waste  
- Zero emissions profile  

### 2.1 Key NEPA Categories & Preliminary Outcome  

| Category | Preliminary Status | Notes |
|---------|--------------------|-------|
| **Land Use** | Low impact | BLM rangeland, minimal competing uses |
| **Wildlife** | Moderate | Elk, deer, pronghorn, small mammals; corridors can be preserved |
| **Vegetation** | Low | Pinyon-juniper & scrub; selective clearing possible |
| **Cultural Resources** | Low-moderate | Requires tribal consultation (Section 106) |
| **Hydrology** | Very low | No major waterways or wetlands |
| **Air Quality** | Very low | Zero emissions site |
| **Noise** | Moderate during construction; low during operations | Launch activity requires safety arc noise modeling |
| **Visual Impact** | Low | Remote site; no wilderness viewshed concerns |
| **Traffic** | Low-moderate | Construction influx manageable with county coordination |

**Finding:**  
A standard **EA → FONSI** (Finding Of No Significant Impact) is achievable with proper planning.

# 4. FAA REVIEW – LAUNCH CORRIDOR & STRUCTURE HEIGHTS  

Your planned mag‑launch system introduces **vertical obstruction** and **trajectory safety** considerations.

### 4.1 FAA Part 77 Airspace Analysis  
- The site is **not under restricted airspace**.  
- No Class B/C/D airspace nearby.  
- Overlying airspace is **Class E starting at 700 ft AGL**, typical for rural Arizona.  
- No nearby commercial airports—Show Low is the closest (∼28 miles).  

### 4.2 Launch System Height  
Estimated launch tube or vertical structure height: **150–300 ft**.  
This WILL require:  
- FAA Form 7460‑1  
- Obstruction evaluation  
- Potential NOTAM issuance during test and launch operations  

### 4.3 Flight Path  
Your launcher is nearly **straight vertical ascent → skip trajectory**, not horizontal like railgun concepts.  
This minimizes lateral range risks.  

**FAA risk:** **Low**, provided filings are maintained and Notice-to-Air Missions (NOTAMs) are coordinated.

# 5. RIGHT-OF-WAY (ROW) & BLM LAND-USE RISKS  

### 5.1 ROW Conflicts  
- Transmission corridor ROW is a benefit, not a hindrance.  
- No known pipeline crossings inside the parcel.  
- Firebreaks must be established around solar fields and hydrogen storage.

### 5.2 BLM Surface Management Considerations  
BLM will evaluate for:  
- Rangeland health  
- Wildlife movement  
- Soil erosion control  
- Public access (which will need to be restricted over time)

### 5.3 Expected Pathway  
A **BLM Right-of-Way Grant** (commercial) or **Land Tenure Adjustment** (rare but possible) is the most likely acquisition path.

# 6. WILDLIFE IMPACT ASSESSMENT  

### 6.1 Species of Interest  
- Mule deer  
- Elk  
- Pronghorn  
- Raptors  
- Migratory birds  

### 6.2 Risks  
Solar arrays pose minor collision hazards for birds, mitigable by panel patterning and anti-reflective coatings.  

Hydrogen storage and launch facilities pose **no biological risk** when properly enclosed.

### 6.3 Mitigation Strategy  
- Preserve **north-south wildlife corridors**.  
- Fence design to include **break points** for large mammal passage.  
- Motion cameras for compliance monitoring.

**Overall wildlife risk:** **Low**.  

---

# 7. FIRE, EMERGENCY RESPONSE & SAFETY ZONES  

### 7.1 Wildfire Considerations  
The region is pine/scrub and seasonally dry.  
Risk mitigations:  
- 100‑ft mineral firebreak around arrays  
- Hydrant or water‑tank‑fed suppression systems  
- Hydrogen tank farm segregation  
- Launch system fire perimeter  

### 7.2 Emergency Access  
All primary roads should be:  
- **20 ft wide**, all‑weather gravel or paved  
- Connected to **two separate evacuation paths**  

Emergency staging areas must be graded near the MECSAI Ops Center.
